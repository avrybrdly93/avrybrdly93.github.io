Research Project

Ball in Flight: The Aerodynamic Effects of Dimples on a Golf Ball

High-fidelity simulation of dimpled vs smooth golf balls, including aerodynamic coefficient fitting, validation, and trajectory analysis.

This independent research project models golf ball flight using spin-dependent lift and drag, turbulent transition effects, and literature-based CL/CD fits. The simulator is validated against indoor launch monitor data and existing experimental studies, and is accompanied by a full paper and reproducible figures.

  • Implements 3D ball flight with drag, lift, and spin decay.
  • Calibrates aerodynamic coefficients using experimental data and literature.
  • Compares dimpled vs smooth balls in terms of carry, peak height, and energy loss.

Physics-Informed Neural Networks for the 2D Wave Equation

Learning wave dynamics directly from the governing PDE using PINNs, with a classical solver for validation and an interactive 3D web demo.

This project couples a finite-difference 2D wave equation solver with a physics-informed neural network that learns the underlying dynamics from data and physical constraints. An interactive Plotly-based visualization and FastAPI backend allow real-time exploration of initial conditions, boundary conditions, and model performance.

  • Implements a stable 2D FD solver satisfying CFL constraints as the reference model.
  • Trains a PINN to minimize PDE residuals and boundary/initial condition errors.
  • Provides a web-based interface for visual comparison: ground truth vs PINN predictions.

About & Academic Focus

I’m especially interested in how careful mathematical modelling and modern machine learning can work together, rather than in isolation, to solve real physical problems.

I completed my B.S. in Mathematics (with a Physics minor) and have since focused on projects that sit at the intersection of numerical analysis, scientific computing, and applied machine learning. From deriving the governing equations and implementing solvers in Python to validating against experimental data and packaging results into clear visualizations, my work includes full-stack simulation projects.

My golf ball aerodynamics project, “Ball in Flight: The Aerodynamic Effects of Dimples on a Golf Ball,” is a central piece of my portfolio. It demonstrates my ability to implement and validate a physically realistic model, perform sensitivity analyses, and communicate results in a formal research paper.

I am now extending this skill set toward scientific machine learning: physics-informed neural networks for PDEs, neural approximations of dynamical systems, and web-based tools that make these models more accessible to others. Alongside my technical work, I have extensive experience teaching mathematics and English, which has cultivated a strong interest in clear explanation and pedagogy.
